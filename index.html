02 · The Bookings Math
$3.5M ÷ $110K ACV ÷ 20% close rate = the floor
  • $3.5M ÷ $110K ACV = 32 closed deals required
  • 32 ÷ 20% close rate = 160 qualified opportunities required
  • 160 × $110K × 5x coverage = $17.6M qualified pipeline needed
  • This is the floor, not the ceiling. The question isn't whether to invest in pipeline generation, it's how efficiently.
Against the alternative of missing the number entirely, the ROI math makes the investment defensible at the corporate level even if Consensus has not yet allocated headroom in the operational budget.
03 · The Throughput Constraint
BDR capacity governs engine sizing and output cadence, not account volume
  • 8 BDRs sustainably absorb 80 to 100 dossier-ready accounts per week
  • ~1,300 to 1,600 dossier-ready accounts across a Q2-Q3 production window
  • 60 AEs comfortably carry the resulting qualified opportunities downstream
  • Engines that produce more than the team can action create waste, not pipeline
Engine sizing is a delivery decision, not a marketing decision. Right-sizing throughput protects both the BDR team's capacity and the integrity of the response rates we'll be measured against.
01 · The Sales Cycle Reality
Cycle length defines which motions can contribute in 2026
  • Net-new Clarity: ~10 month cycle. Started in 2026 produces 2027 bookings, not 2026
  • Channel co-sell into healthcare integrators: 2 to 6 month cycle on in-flight projects
  • Clarity expansion into existing eFax customers: 2 to 4 month cycle, MSAs already in place
  • This is structural reality, not a function of vendor speed or budget
2026 bookings require expansion and channel motions. Net-new motions still matter, but as 2027 pre-load, not 2026 contributors. Confusing the two is how revenue plans miss.
04 · Healthcare Segmentation Complexity
Each sub-vertical requires its own custom-built infrastructure
  • Sub-vertical-specific signal library and scoring model
  • Three-tier ICP contact map across C-suite, IT leadership, and EMR/HIM operational personas
  • Buying centers, pain points, and conversion drivers vary materially by sub-vertical
  • Foundation, sub-vertical builds, contact mapping, and BDR enablement are sequential dependencies measured in weeks
Healthcare data requirements also eliminate offshoring as a viable execution path, which has cost and quality implications most agencies don't account for.
